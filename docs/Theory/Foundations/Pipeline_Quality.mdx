:::tip Definition
**Data Pipeline Quality** refers to the practices, rules, and controls that ensure data is **accurate, complete, consistent, timely, and fit for purpose** as it flows through ingestion, transformation, storage, and consumption layers.
:::

Data quality is not a single check — it is a **system of guarantees** spanning:

- Quality dimensions
- Validation rules
- Pipeline behaviour
- Observability
- Governance and ownership
- Lineage and metadata
- Incident response

---

## 🎯 What Problem Does This Solve?

High‑quality data pipelines enable:

| Benefit | Why it matters |
|--------|----------------|
| **Trustworthy data** | Decisions rely on accurate, complete, consistent inputs |
| **Reliable pipelines** | Failures are detected early and handled predictably |
| **Stable downstream systems** | Prevents breakage in dashboards, ML models, and APIs |
| **Faster debugging** | Clear lineage and observability reduce MTTR |
| **Governance & accountability** | Ownership and SLAs ensure long‑term quality |

Poor quality pipelines create silent failures, incorrect metrics, and business risk.

---

## 🧠 Conceptual Model (How Data Quality Works)

Data quality spans **four layers**:

### **1. Structural Quality**
Does the data conform to expected schema and types?

### **2. Semantic Quality**
Does the data make sense in the business domain?

### **3. Operational Quality**
Is the pipeline behaving correctly under load and over time?

### **4. Organisational Quality**
Are ownership, SLAs, and governance clearly defined?

These layers reinforce each other — a pipeline is only as strong as its weakest dimension.

---

# 🧩 Quality Dimensions (Foundational)

### **Accuracy**
Data correctly represents real‑world events or entities.

### **Completeness**
Required data is present (no missing keys, no partial ingestion).

### **Consistency**
Data does not conflict across systems or time.

### **Timeliness**
Data arrives within expected SLAs.

### **Validity**
Data conforms to expected formats, ranges, and allowed values.

These dimensions form the **baseline trust contract** for any dataset.

---

# 🧬 Structural & Schema Quality

Ensures data adheres to expected technical structure.

### **Schema Enforcement**
Detects or blocks unexpected schema changes.

### **Data Types & Constraints**
Ensures values match declared types and constraints.

### **Referential Integrity**
Validates relationships between datasets (e.g., foreign keys resolve).

### **Evolution Rules**
Defines how schemas may change without breaking consumers.

**TA Lens:**
Structural issues often cause pipeline breakage, not silent errors.

---

# 🔧 Operational Quality

Day‑to‑day checks that ensure pipelines behave as expected.

### **Freshness**
Detects delayed or stalled data updates.

### **Volume & Distribution**
Ensures record counts and value distributions fall within expected bounds.

### **Anomaly Detection**
Flags unexpected changes in metrics or patterns.

### **Idempotency**
Ensures reprocessing does not create duplicates or inconsistencies.

### **Error Handling**
Defines how malformed or invalid records are handled.

**TA Lens:**
Operational quality reveals pipeline health, not just data correctness.

---

# 🧠 Business & Semantic Quality

Ensures data aligns with business meaning.

### **Business Rules**
Domain‑specific validation (e.g., order value > 0).

### **Metric Definitions**
Ensures metrics are calculated consistently across teams.

### **Domain Constraints**
Industry‑specific rules (e.g., valid lifecycle states).

### **Cross‑Dataset Consistency**
Ensures metrics align across sources.

**TA Lens:**
Semantic quality is where most “silent errors” originate.

---

# 🔄 Pipeline & Processing Quality

Protects data quality during ingestion and transformation.

### **Ingestion Validation**
Rejects malformed or incomplete records at entry points.

### **Transformation Integrity**
Ensures transformations do not introduce loss or distortion.

### **Deduplication**
Prevents duplicate outputs during retries or reprocessing.

### **Ordering Guarantees**
Ensures events or records are processed in the correct order.

### **Checkpointing & Recovery**
Prevents partial writes or inconsistent states.

**TA Lens:**
Pipeline quality is about *behaviour*, not just data.

---

# 📡 Monitoring & Observability

Quality requires visibility.

### **Data Profiling**
Baseline distributions, ranges, and patterns.

### **Freshness Checks**
Detects late or missing data.

### **Volume Monitoring**
Tracks record counts and anomalies.

### **Drift Detection**
Identifies changes in schema, distribution, or semantics.

### **Alerting**
Notifies owners when quality thresholds are breached.

**TA Lens:**
Observability is the difference between *knowing* and *guessing*.

---

# 🏛️ Cross‑Cutting Quality Capabilities

### **Governance & Ownership**
Clear responsibility for datasets and pipelines.

### **Metadata & Lineage**
Tracks where data came from, how it was transformed, and who owns it.

### **Data Quality SLAs**
Defines expectations for freshness, accuracy, and availability.

### **Incident Management**
Structured response to quality failures.

### **Documentation**
Clear definitions, schemas, and expectations.

**TA Lens:**
Quality is not just technical — it is organisational.

# 🧩 Quality Decision Map

| Problem Shape | Quality Focus |
|---------------|---------------|
| Missing or late data | Freshness, SLAs, ingestion checks |
| Incorrect metrics | Semantic rules, metric definitions |
| Schema breakage | Structural quality, schema enforcement |
| Duplicate records | Idempotency, deduplication |
| Unexpected spikes/drops | Volume monitoring, anomaly detection |
| Conflicting values | Consistency, referential integrity |

| Output Type | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Quality reports** | Summaries of checks, failures, and trends |
| **Alerts** | Notifications for SLA breaches or anomalies |
| **Lineage graphs** | Visualisation of data flow and dependencies |
| **Validation logs** | Records of rejected or malformed data |
| **Quality dashboards** | Freshness, volume, and distribution metrics |

---

####  ⚠️ Common Mistakes

- Treating data quality as a “final step” instead of a system
- Relying solely on schema validation
- Assuming pipelines are idempotent when they aren’t
- Ignoring semantic quality in favour of structural checks
- No clear ownership for datasets
- No SLAs for freshness or accuracy
- Silent failures due to missing observability
