## 🧠 Theory
This **Theory** space captures the fundamental concepts, mechanisms, and system behaviours that underpin modern software, data, and distributed architectures.
Where **Workbench** describes the mediums you think in, **Runbook** describes the tools you operate, and **Projects** show how you applied them — **Theory explains the underlying forces that shape all of them.**

Theory pages answer questions like:

- *What is this concept?*
- *Why does it exist?*
- *How does it behave under load, scale, or failure?*
- *What trade‑offs does it introduce?*
- *How does it interact with other parts of a system?*

This section is your **conceptual foundation** — the mental models that make you a stronger Technical Analyst.
It helps you:

- understand how systems behave in real conditions
- anticipate bottlenecks, risks, and failure modes
- reason about architecture and design choices
- communicate clearly with engineers and stakeholders
- evaluate solutions with precision and confidence

If Workbench is *how you think*, Theory is *what you think with* — the durable knowledge that makes your analysis sharper, your designs cleaner, and your decisions more grounded.
