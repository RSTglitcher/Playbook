What this means in practice:
You consistently operate at the system and platform level rather than treating work as isolated tickets or features.
Evidence from your work:
	- DataHub / Global Data Catalogue: Treated metadata not as an add‑on, but as a foundational platform capability, spanning discovery, lineage, ownership, governance, and self‑service over a multi‑year roadmap.
	- Tech Backlog Formalisation: Reframed the backlog from a “list of tickets” into a decision system, analysing behaviour, flow, and incentives rather than just volume.
	- Centralised Documentation: Designed documentation as a product and platform surface, not a collection of pages, with ingestion, ownership, and lifecycle explicitly modelled.

✅ This is senior‑level behaviour: seeing structures, feedback loops, and incentives, not just outputs.


### 🔹 Problem Framing & Root‑Cause Analysis
What this means in practice:
You consistently redefine problems so teams solve the right issue, not just a visible symptom.
Evidence from your work:
	- Tech Backlog: Demonstrated (with data) that the core issue was not backlog size, but permissive intake, missing ownership, and lack of commitment signals.
	- Caching (Boomerang): Prevented premature architectural decisions by separating:
		- BAU vs binge traffic
		- local vs distributed cache concerns
		- incident mitigation vs long‑term platform strategy
	- OpenAPI standardisation: Identified that the real risk was contract drift and fragmented sources of truth—not lack of tooling.

✅ This shows mature analytical judgement and an ability to reduce wasted effort.


### 🔹 Requirements Formation & Decision Enablement
What this means in practice:
You turn ambiguity into something teams can decide and act on.
Evidence from your work:
	- DataHub / GDC: Produced sustained requirements and planning artefacts covering:
		- producer vs consumer responsibilities
		- metadata entities and ownership
		- phased capability delivery (discovery → lineage → self‑service feeds → governance)
	- Boomerang work (Caching, OpenAPI, Datadog): Translated incidents and friction into:
		- explicit questions
		- trade‑off tables
		- alignment workshops
	- Centralised Documentation: Defined clear “author vs consumer vs catalogue” boundaries, preventing documentation debt from re‑emerging.

✅ This demonstrates high‑quality TA craft: requirements as decision tools, not paperwork. Turning ambiguity into actionable plans

### 🔹 Data‑Driven Analysis & Evidence‑Led Storytelling
What this means in practice:
You use data to change minds, not just report metrics.
Evidence from your work:

✅ This data to drive organizational change is a strong marker of senior‑level analytical influence.
