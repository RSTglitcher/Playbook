## **📂 Projects**
This **Projects** space captures the real work you’ve delivered — the initiatives, investigations, and solutions where your Technical Analyst skills were applied to create measurable value.
Where **Theory** explains how systems work, **Workbench** explains how you think, and **Runbook** explains how tools behave — **Projects show what you actually did with all of that knowledge.**

Project pages answer questions like:

- *What was the problem? Why did it matter?*
- *What decisions did you make and why?*
- *What analysis, modelling, or investigation did you perform?*
- *What artefacts did you produce?*
- *What value did you deliver to the organisation?*

This section is your **evidence base** — the concrete, demonstrable record of your impact.
It helps you:

- articulate your contributions clearly and confidently
- demonstrate breadth and depth across skills, tools, and domains
- show hiring managers and stakeholders how you think and operate
- build a reusable portfolio of patterns, decisions, and artefacts
- reflect on your growth and refine your analytical method

If Workbench is *how you think*, Theory is *what you understand*, and Runbook is *how you operate*,
**Projects are the proof — the lived application of your method, your reasoning, and your craft.**
