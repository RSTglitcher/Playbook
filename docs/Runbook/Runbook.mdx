## **📘 Runbook**
This **Runbook** space captures the operational behaviour of the tools, platforms, and systems you interact with day‑to‑day.
Where **Theory** explains how systems work and **Workbench** explains how you think, **Runbook explains how tools behave in the real world — and how to work with them safely, effectively, and diagnostically.**

Runbook pages answer questions like:

- *What does this tool do and how does it work?*
- *How do I access it, configure it, or authenticate with it?*
- *What does “normal” look like? What does “broken” look like?*
- *How do I triage issues quickly and propose the right actions?*
- *What does a Technical Analyst need to know before using this tool?*

This section is your **operational muscle memory** — the practical knowledge that lets you move confidently across platforms, environments, and systems.
It helps you:

- understand how tools behave under normal and abnormal conditions
- navigate credentials, permissions, and access patterns
- interpret logs, metrics, and system outputs
- diagnose issues and propose credible next steps
- collaborate effectively with engineers, SREs, and platform teams

If Theory is *why systems behave the way they do*, and Workbench is *how you think*,
**Runbook is how you *operate* — the lived, practical interface between you and the technical world.**
