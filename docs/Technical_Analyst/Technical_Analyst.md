<TabItem value="Questions" label="10 Questions">

### **1. How do they structure and approach technical problems?**

- Observe: How do they break down complex issues before writing code or documentation?
- Milestone evidence: You can clearly articulate **their problem-solving framework** and apply it to at least one real issue.

### **2. What tools, scripts, or shortcuts do they rely on for efficiency?**

- Look for: Custom scripts, automation, debugging tricks, terminal commands, or IDE setups.
- Milestone evidence: You’ve integrated at least 3 of their tools into your workflow and can explain **why and when to use them**.

### **3. How do they maintain high-quality, readable, and maintainable code or specs?**

- Observe: Formatting, naming conventions, documentation style, and modularity.
- Milestone evidence: You can submit a piece of work following their standards **without major corrections**.

### **4. How do they validate assumptions or verify correctness?**

- Observe: Testing methods, logging, checks, and verification workflows.
- Milestone evidence: You can independently **validate a non-trivial feature or fix** using their methods.

### **5. How do they manage information and knowledge?**

- Look for: Note-taking, organizing documentation, versioning, or reference habits.
- Milestone evidence: You’ve created your **own organized system** based on their approach and can retrieve info efficiently.

### **6. How do they communicate complex technical concepts to non-technical stakeholders?**

- Observe: Explanations, visuals, analogies, or email style.
- Milestone evidence: You’ve successfully presented a technical point to a stakeholder using **their communication techniques**.

### **7. How do they prioritize tasks and handle multiple competing deadlines?**

- Look for: Task management, sprint planning, personal productivity habits.
- Milestone evidence: You can **plan a week of work like they do** without dropping tasks or producing low-quality outputs.

### **8. What patterns or practices do they follow for long-term maintainability?**

- Observe: Modular code, reusable components, templates, or documentation standards.
- Milestone evidence: You’ve contributed **something reusable or maintainable** that could be used by others.

### **9. How do they approach learning new technologies or unfamiliar problems?**

- Observe: Self-directed research, asking questions, experiments, and iteration.
- Milestone evidence: You can **independently solve a problem outside your comfort zone** using their learning approach.

### **10. What professional habits set them apart?**

- Look for: Attention to detail, decision-making speed, collaboration, reviewing others’ work, or giving/receiving feedback.
- Milestone evidence: You can identify **3 habits to emulate** and have integrated at least one into your daily workflow.
